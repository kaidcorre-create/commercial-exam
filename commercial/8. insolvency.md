### what
- when a company does not have enough money to pay its debt
	- debtor cannot meet obligations to creditors
- english law always for rescue and rehabilitation, not just liquidation
- the word insolvency can relate to both an event and a procedure
	- debtor lacks means
	- procedure to wind up, rescue, or restructure the debtor
creditor resolution area
- aims for a fair an collective distribution of assets
- prevents disorderly enforcement of claims
- aims to balance creditor recovery and rescue of distressed entities


types
- cash flow insolvent
	- IA 1986, s.123(1)(e)
	- debotr unable to pay debts that fall due
	- [[BNY Corporate Trustees v Eurosail [2013] UKSC 28]] - assets dont matter
- balacne sheet 
	- IA 1986, s.123(2)
	- liabilities excees assets, inclusing contingent and prospective obligatiopns
	- difference between temporary liquidity issues and fundamnetal insolvency


procedures
- liquidation (winding up)
	- terminal procedure, company ceases to exist
	- office holder: liquidator
	- purpose: maximise returns to creditors and dissolve the compnay
- administration
	- rescue/restructre distressed companies
	- office holder: adminstrator - takes control from directors
	- focus: continue trading, preserving valiue, maximising collectove creditor returns
	- streamlined by enterprise act 2002
- CVA
	- formal agreement between compnay and creditors to compromise debts
	- office holder: supervisor, ensuring compliance with CVA terms 
	- benefits: avoids liquidation, enables business survival, provides strcutured repayment iption
- moratorium (CIGA 2020)
	- temporary protection from enforcemnt by creditos
	- office holder: monitor, oversees the process
	- enables restructuring or refinancing without immediate creditor pressure



core fuctions of insolvency
- maximisinf returns to creditors
	- west mercia saftywear ltd v dodd
	- bti 2014 llc v sequana sa
- fair ranking of claims
	- insolvencey act 1986, s175 & sch 6
- facilitating rescue
	- IA 1986, sch b1 para 3
	- coporate insolvency and governence act 2020
- policy mismanagement
	- IA 1986, s213-214
- suspension of individual enforcement
	- Re atlantic computer systems plc



Ayerst (Inspector of Taxes) v C & k (Construction) Ltd [1976]
- comapny assets are held on trust upon insolvency - for creditors
- assets must be dealt woth in accordance with insolvenccy legislation
	- assets are therfore no longer freely disposable
	- they must be ised only for statutory purposes
- assets therefore only avialble if the company has benefical ownerhsip
	- property exluded
		- retention of title goods (aluminium industrie vaassen bv v romaloha aluminium kts)
		- trust property
		- valid sectruity intersts

role of offce holder
- act in creditors interests
- respect existing propreitary. rights
- take assets "as found"
	- does not improve the comapnies preperty rights
