### arbitration
- a process by which parties consensually submit a dispute to a non-governmental decision-maker, selected by or for the parties, to render a binding decision resolving a dispute in accordance with neutral, adjudicatory procedures affording each party an opportunity to present its case
	- must comply with minimum procedures of natural justice
	- provides alternative to litigation
	- outside of the judicial system
	- there are ad hoc and institutional arbitration
- key characteristics
	- ==consent==
		- comes out of contract - arbitration agreement
		- the agreement may be in writing as a clause of the international commerical contract or evidenced in writing/electronic communications
			- section 5 arbitration act 1996
			- article 2 NY convention
		- post dispute agreement is called a submission agreement
	- ==non governmental decision maker selected by an for the parties==
		- if a governmental decision maker is selected, then it is a choice of court agreement, not an arbitration
		- the emphasis on the dispute resolution needs of the parties as opposed to public policy or interest
		- non lawyers can be arbitrators but arbitrators have to be independant and impartial
			- but mainly from the commercial bar
	- ==a final and binding decision==
		- it is not for advisory recommendations
		- voluntary compliance (can be coercively enforced by state courts) - cannot be enforced
		- no general appeal to a higher level court  - article 69(1) Arbitration Act 1996
		- non contractual disputes cannot be decided in - arbiter wpuld be exceeding jurisdiction - decided by terms of reference in the arbitration agreement
		- s.24 arbitration act 1996 - procedual appeal, can remove an arbitrator on the gorunds of bias
		- facts finding are binding, but law may not be
			- can contract out of this right to appeal
	- ==adjudicatory procedure==
		- can appraoch high court to challeneg appointement of arbitrator 
			- on grounds of bias or conflict of interest
			- as oer arbitration act
- advanatges
	- international forum
		- neutral
		- no home advantage
