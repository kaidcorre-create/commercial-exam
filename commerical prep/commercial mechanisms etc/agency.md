## how is agency created? #card 
- by agreement
- through ratification
- through apparent or ostensible authority

## when can an agent be sued on the contract? #card 
- when there is an undisclosed principal
^1776549087799



## how is agency created by agreement? #card 
- express agreement - normal contract rules apply
- implied agreement - Waugh v HB Clifford & Sons
- usual course of business/custom - Watteau v Fenwick
^1776549087802


## when is ratification possible? #card 
- the grant must purport to act for a named and identifiable principal (must be disclosed)
- the principal must have actually existed and been ascertainable at the time the contract was made - Keighley, Maxstead & CO v Durant; Kelner v Baxter
- the principal must have the necessary capacity to make that contract - Firth v Staines
^1776549087804


## ratification must be: #card
- timely - Re Portuguese Consolidated Copper Mines
- not ratifying a nullity -  Danish Mercantile Co v Beaumont
- where the principal has full knowledge of the facts
- either by words or by conduct - does not need to be communicated to the third party - The Kilmun
^1776549087807



## when is does apparent authority occur? #card 
-  a representation must have been made that the person had authority to act as an agent - Summers v Solomon
- the representation must be one of fact, not law - Kleinwort Benson v Lincoln City Council
- the representation must have been made by the principal to the third party - The Ocean Frost; Dickson v Valpy
- the third party must have relied on the representation - The Ocean Frost; Jacobs v Morris
- BUT even if the above are not present,  principal may be estopped by his own subsequent conduct from denying the agent’s authority if the third party has relied on that conduct -Spiro v. Lintern
^1776549087812


## when may apparent authority be vitiated? #card 
- where a forger has been held out as having authority to conduct the type of transaction in question - Uxbridge Permanent Benefit Building Society v. Pickard
^1776549087815


## when might agency be created by necessity? #card
- only practicable action - Couturier v Hastie; Prager v Blatspiel; Stam v Heacock Ltd
- must have been no opportunity to get instructions from owner/principal - Springer v Great Western Railway
- must have acted honestly and in the owners interests
^1776549087818


## how is agency terminated consensually? #card 
- performance of obligations - Blackburn v Scholes
- fixed term expires - Dickinson v Lilwal
- notice, effective even if breach of contract arises - Decro-Wall International SA v Practitioners in Marketing Ltd
- inconsistent conduct - Nelson v Rolfe
- insanity - Yonge v Toynbee
- death of either party
- dissolution of company or partnership
- cessation or transfer of business - Rhodes v Forward; Turner v Goldsmith
- brankruptcy 
^1776549087820

## how is agency terminated non-consensually? #card 
- to defeat usual or apparent authority 
	- third parties must be notified - Summers v Solomon
	- notification even in cases of insanity - Drew v Nunn
^1776549087823


## when can agency not be terminated? #card
- agency coupled with an interest cannot be terminated
- where the agent has become personally liable - Seymour v Bridge
- where there is an enduring power of attorney
^1776549087825



## what are the duties of an agent? #card
- to perform the contract - Turpin v Bilton (unless its illegal/null - Cohen v Kittell)
- to be obedient to the principle - Volkers v Midland Doherty (but not required to act illegally - Bexwell v Christie)
- to exercise due care and skill - Chaudry v Prabhakar; Lambert v Heath
- not to delegate - Allam & Co v Europa Poster Services
- to not deny the principal's title - Dixon v Hamond
- to account
- fidelity - McPherson v Watt
- not to make secret profits - Boardman v Phipps
^1776549087828

## what are the duties of principals? #card 
- to remunerate
- to indemnify
^1776549087830





## what is a commercial agent? #card 
- created by an EU directive
- attempt to define, formalise and protect self employed commercial agents
^1776549087832


## what does reg 3(1) say? #card 
- 3(1) requires agents to "look after the interests of his principal and acts dutifully in good faith"
^1776549087835


## what does reg 3(2) say? #card
-  "make proper efforts to negotiate and conclude the transactions"
	- keep the principal informed
	- comply with principals reasonable instructions
^1776549087837


## what does reg 4(2) say? #card 
 - reg. 4(2)(b))
	- to obtain all necessary information and communicate it to the agent 
	- to give reasonable notice to the agent if the volume of transactions is anticipate
^1776549087839


## what does reg 4(3) say? #card 
- to give timely notification to the agent of any decision to accept or decline to execute any transaction procured by the agent 
^1776549087841


## what does reg 10(3) say? #card
- to pay commission promptly, that is, not later than the last day of the month following the quarter in which the commission became due 
	- by the same date, to provide a statement of commission due, including the main components used in calculating the commission.
	- to supply to the agent, if demanded, all information, including extracts from the principal’s books, necessary to allow the agent to check the amount of commission due to him.
^1776549087845




## how is a commercial agency terminated? #card 
- commerical agency for a fixed term will end when the term comes to an end, unless both parties carry on performing
	- When it comes to termination, it is obvious that this might be for a variety of reasons: 
		- a fixed-term agency has come to its end;
		- an agency contract for an indefinite period is terminated by notice; 
		- the agency has been terminated by the principal for good cause; the agency has been terminated by the agent for good cause; the principal or agent has died or become insolvent or otherwise ceased trading; the agency has been wrongly terminated.  
^1776549087847
