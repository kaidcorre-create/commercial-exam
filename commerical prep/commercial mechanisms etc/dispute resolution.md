## what is arbitration? #card 
- parties consensually submit dispute to non-governmental decision makers
- final and binding decision on the merits
^1777316430245


## 4 key characteristics of arbitration #card
- consent
- non governmental decision maker
- final and binding decision
- adjudicatory procedure
^1777316430252


## consent #card 
- arbitration agreement
- must be in writing or evidenced
- may be a clause in the original commercial agreement or post dispute (submission agreement)
^1777316430255


## non governmental decision maker #card 
- independent and impartial
- nemo iudex in sua causa (non bias)
- audi alteram partem (fair hearing)
^1777316430257


## final and binding decision #card 
- not merely advisory, not a negotiated settlement
- no right of appeal on the law but this can be contracted out of
- facts are binding
- decision can be challenged on procedure/bias
- non contractual disputes cannot be decided
^1777316430259



## ad hoc arbitration #card 
- no institutions, parties agree procedure
- maximum procedural flexibility
- governed by national law of seat only
^1777316430261


## institutional arbitration #card 
- LCIA, ICC, SIAC
- institutional rules apply
- fees payable to institutions
- has to be set out in clause
^1777316430264


## arbitration clauses #card 
- arising under - narrow
- arising out of or in connection with - broad 
- both types construed broadly. rational parties intend all disputes from their relationship to go to the same tribunal - Fiona Trust v Privalov 
^1777316430266
