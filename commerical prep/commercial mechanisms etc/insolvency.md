## what is insolvency? #card 
- an event - company unable to meet debt obligations
- set of procedures - rescue, rehabilitation and liquidation
^1777313801845



## 2 insolvency tests #card 
- cash flow test - s.123(1)(e)
- balance sheet test - s.123(2)
^1777313801849


## cash flow test #card
- debtor unable to pay debts as they fall due
- even if assets are sufficient - BNY Corporate Trustees v Eurosail
- temporary liquidity issues usually require administration (rescue)
^1777313801851


## balance sheet test #card
- liabilities exceed assets
- focus on long-term financial health
- fundamental insolvency leading to liquidation
^1777313801854


## 4 insolvency procedures #card 
- liquidation/winding up
- administration
- CVA
- moratorium
^1777313801856


## liquidation #card 
- officer holder - liquidator
- gather and maximise assets
^1777313801858


## administration #card 
- officer holder - administrator
- rescue company as going concern
- better result for creditors than winding up
- realise assets for secured/preferential creditors
^1777313801860


## CVA #card 
- officer holder - supervisor
- agreed compromise
- structured repayment plan
^1777313801865


## Moratorium #card 
- officer holder - monitor
- enables for restructuring/refinancing without immediate creditor pressure
^1777313801867



## hierarchy of creditor priority #card 
- (1) Fixed charge creditors.
- (2) Expenses of the insolvency proceedings.
- (3) Preferential creditors.
- (4) Floating charge creditors.
- (5) Unsecured provable debts.
- (6) Statutory interest.
- (7) Non-provable liabilities.
^1777313801871


## fixed charge creditor #card 
- over specified assets
- assets cannot be disposed of without consent
- must register within 21 days of creation
- highest priority
^1777313801874


## floating charge creditor #card 
- over a class of changing assets (stock)
- becomes fixed on insolvency or other trigger event
- must register within 21 days
^1777313801875



## pari passu #card
- s.107
- creditors in the same category share assets proportionately to the size of their claim
- cannot contract to put yourself in a more favourable position
- can contract to put yourself in a less favourable posistion
^1777313801878


## anti-deprivation rule #card 
- assets cannot be removed from the company through contractual arrangements that favour one creditor over the collective interests of all creditors
^1777313801880


## directors insolvency duties #card 
- must prioritise creditors interests when insolvency is imminent
- director liable if knew or ought to have known about insolvency and continued to trade without steps minimising creditor loss
- trading with intent to defraud creditors gives rise to criminal sanctions and personal liability
^1777313801882


## administration objectives #card 
- rescue company as a going concern
- achieve better result for creditors than winding up would
- realise property to make distribution to secured/preferential creditors
^1777313801884


## void and voidable transactions #card 
- s.127 - disposing of company property after winding up petition filed are void transactions
- s.238 - undervalue transactions within 2 years if with a connected person or 6 months if not can be set aside unless for a genuine commercial purpose
- s.239 - paying one creditor over others when company is insolvent is voidable - within 6 months if unconnected, 2 years if connected persons
- clawback - officeholder can investigate and clawback improper transactions from 2 years prior to insolvency date
^1777313801887
