## bill of lading #card

## seaway bill #card

## what is the difference between a bill of lading to order and a straight bill of lading? #card
- to order is negotiable (transferrable)

^1776442574392


## life cycle/flow of carriage of goods by sea #card 
- shipper books cargo and delivers goods to carrier
- carrier issues bill of lading
- carrier gives 3 bill of ladings to the shipper
- shipper endorses and sends one to buyer
- buyer/lawful holder presents at least one endorsed original to carrier
- carrier verifies bill of lading and holders legal title
- carrier stamps bill "ACCOMPLISHED"
- carrier issues a delivery order
^1776442574393

## what are the hague - visby rules about? #card 
- govern the rights and liabilities of carriers and shippers
- rules contain:
	- carriers obligations
	- carriers exceptions from liability
	- shippers responsibilities
	- extent of carriers liability to shipper
	- the amounts recoverable from the carrier in case of loss or damage to the goods whilst in the carriers care
^1776442574396

## when do the hague - visby rules apply? #card 
- bills of lading issued in contracting states
- carriage from a contracting state
- if incorporated into the bill of lading
^1776442574397


## what are the obligations of the carrier under the hague - visby rules? #card
- to ensure the vessel’s seaworthiness
- to care for the cargo
- to proceed with reasonable dispatch
- to carry the goods without unjustifiable delay
- to carry the goods without deviation
- to issue a bill of lading where the shipper requests one.
^1776442574398

## what is article 3 rule 1 hv rules? #card
- carriers obligations regarding seaworthiness (no breach for latent defects)
	- must make the ship sea worthy (at the start)
	- must properly man, equip and supply the ship
	- make the parts of the ship in which goods are to be carried, fit and safe for their reception, carriage and preservation
^1776442574399



## what is article 3 rule 2 hv rules? #card
- care for cargo, carrier shall properly and carefully:
	- load
	- handle
	- stow
	- carry
	- keep care for
	- and discharge the goods carried
^1776442574400


## what is article 4 rule 4 hv rules? #card
- carriage without unreasonable deviation
	- carrier liable for loss unless:
	- authorised deviation
	- circumstances beyond reasonable control
	- reasonable necessary for compliance with an express or implied warranty
	- reasonable necessity to save ship or its cargo
	- saving human life or ship in distress
	- medical or surgical aid for a person
	- barratrous conduct of master or crew
	- industrial action unconnected with carriers unreasonable conduct
^1776442574401

## what are the expected perils? #card 
- Carrier will not be responsible for loss or damage arising from:
	- An act, neglect or fault of the master, mariner, pilot or the servants of the carrier in the navigation and management of the ship.
	- Fire unless caused by actual fault of carrier.
	- Perils, dangers and accidents of sea.
	- Acts of God, war, public enemies.
	- Arrest or restrain of princes, rulers.
	- Act or omission of shipper or his agents.
	- Strikes lockouts etc.
	- Riots and civil commotions.
	- Saving or attempting to save life or property at sea.
	- Wastage of cargo due to its inherent qualities, insufficient packing marks etc.
	- Latent defects not discoverable by due diligence.
	- Any other cause arising without the actual fault of carrier.
^1776442574406
