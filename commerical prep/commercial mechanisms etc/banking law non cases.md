## what is a bank? #card
- no single definition
- accept deposits, honour and cash cheques - Dominions Trust v Kirkwood
- authorised institution under FMSA 2000 - Banking Act 2009
^1777302447373



## who regulates banks? #card
- Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) - customer protection, competition, fairness
- Prudential Regulation Authority (PRA) - liquidity and risk allocation
^1777302447379


## who is a customer? #card 
- person who opens a bank
- relationship begins when bank agrees to open an account - Woods v Martins Bank
^1777302447383


## 7 duties owed to customers #card 
- duty to pay on demand
- duty to follow customers mandate
- duty not to exceed mandate
- duty to obey countermand
- duty to collect instruments
- duty to render account/provide instruments
- duty of confidentiality 
^1777302447385



## duty to pay on demand #card 
- bank must pay when customer validly demands so
- failure to do so is a breach of contract
^1777302447388

## duty to follow customers mandate #card 
- refusal to follow instructions is a breach - Sierra Leone Telecom v Barclays Bank
- must clarify if instructions are unclear - Cooper v National Westminster Bank
^1777302447390

## 5 grounds of refusal of customer mandate #card 
- insufficient funds
- unclear/ambiguous instructions - London Joint Stock Bank v Macmillan
- reasonable suspicion of fraud - Lipkin Gorman v Karpnale
- stale cheque
- restricted by law
^1777302447393


## duty to obey countermand #card
- s.75 Bill of Exchange Act 1882
- must be:
	- clear, 
	- unambiguous
	- and received in time
^1777302447395


## duty to collect instruments #card
- must exercise reasonable care - Marfani v Midland Bank
	- bank not liable for conversion if acting in good faith and without negligence
^1777302447397


## duty of confidentiality #card
- implied duty not to disclose customer information - Tournier v National Provincial & Union Bank
^1777302447399

## 4 exceptions to duty of confidentiality #card 
- required by law
- required by duty to the public
- necessary to protect interests of bank
- if its made with the cutomers express of implied consent
^1777302447401


## duties owed to banks #card
- draw cheques with reasonable care
- notify bank of forgery promptly 
^1777302447403


## setting off #card 
- bank entitled to apply credit balance in one account against the debit balance in another (must be the same customer, acting in the same capacity)
- no consent required - Stoney Stanton Supplies v Midland Bank
^1777302447405


## types of bank #card 
- retail - day to day banking
- commercial - trade finance, credit facilities etc
- investment - mergers and acquisitions, large-scale investments
- building societies - owned by members, no shareholders, more risk averse
- challenger banks - digital
^1777302447409
