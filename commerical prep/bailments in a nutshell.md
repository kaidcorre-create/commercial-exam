

## general principles
- only personal, tangible property can be bailed
- bailment can change its nature overtime. type of bailment may change
- types of bailment are as follows:
	- depositum
		- goods delivered to another to keep without fee
	- commodatum
		- lending something for the use of the borrower, without charge
	- locatio et condictio
		- hiring goods to another
	- vadium
		- pledging goods (pawn shop)
	- locatio operis faciendi
		- goods delivered to another for something to be done to them
		- goods delivered to another to transport
	- mandatum
		- goods delivered to another to be conveyed, or for a particular purpose, wthout hire
	- locatio custodiae
		- hire of custody. goods bailed to another who is paid to look after them
	- constructive bailments
		- incl non consensual bailments (theft)
	- odd cases
		- conditional sale 


## negligence and bailments
- relationship of bailment proves proximity and establishes duty of care
- standard of care is ordinary standard of reasonable care
- exception to rules:
	- no defence of *jus tertii* (third party with superior title)
	- in locatio operis faciendi, no requirment to even prove negligence - Chapman v. Great Western Rly
	- liability for crimes of third parties - BRS v Arthur V Crutchley Ltd
	- liability for actions of servants/contractors - BRS v Arthur V Crutchley Ltd, Port Swettenham Authority v. TW Wu & Co
	- sub bailees can be sued directly - the pioneer container
	- normal burden of proof is reversed - Travers & Sons Ltd v. Cooper


## breach of contract and bailments
- same rules as are normal in contract
- there may be exclusion/limitation clauses
- (pioneer container) 
	- sub-bailment authorised on any terms → sub-bailee's terms bind bailor
	- no direct contract needed
	- tort claim won't bypass this
	- result is a chain of contracts, each with own terms


## tover (conversion) and bailments
- treating goods as if they are your own
- abuse of possession
	- innocent receipt from rogue - hollins v fowler
	- risk-taking behaviour - moorgate mercantile v finch
	- wrongful sale/disposal - lancashire waggin co v fitzhugh
		- indivisible bulk, victim entitled to full ownership - jones v de marchant
		- divisible bulk, victim entitled to % of ownership - Indian Oil Corp. Ltd v. Greenstone Shipping SA "Ypatianna".
- demand for goods, met by refusal to deliver
	- 
- original bailee is liable for acts of servants, sub-bailees, servants/contractors of sub-bailees etc - morris v cw martin & sons ltd
- must have immediate right to possession

## trespass and bailments
- deliberate act
- 6 year limitation period
- not all trespass to goods is a trover
- must have immediate right to possession

## orders the court can make
- breach of contract - damages 
	- usual rules - hadley v baxendale
	- same terms govern - liquidated damages, exclusion, limitation etc
- in negligence
	- same rules apply on quantum of damages
	- different rules apply regarding liability
	- no but for test
- trespass - damages
- trover - damages and remedies *in rem*
