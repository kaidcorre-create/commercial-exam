## S.3 Torts (Interference with Goods) Act 1977  #card
- Sets out remedies available for wrongful interference with goods
- **Remedies:**
    - Delivery of the goods plus damages
    - Delivery of goods with no damages
    - Damages alone (value of goods + any consequential loss)
- Court has discretion as to which remedy is ordered
- Claimant may have a choice between delivery and damages depending on circumstances
^1777153154270

## S.4 Torts (Interference with Goods) Act 1977  #card
- Allows a bailee to apply to court for relief where the bailor cannot be found or refuses to collect goods
- Protects bailees who are stuck with goods they cannot return
- Bailee can seek an order authorising sale or disposal of the goods
- Prevents bailee being indefinitely liable for goods the bailor won't take back
^1777153154272

## Khan v. Grocutt [2002] EWCA Civ 1945 #card
- car hire split between khan and grocutt. khan took custody of documents
- involved in a collision, khan gives docs to turkish police
- passenger sues grocutt 
- grocutt couldnt produce cert of insurance
- grocutt sues khan for costs as it was his fault there was no insurance doc
- khan had duty of care to care for the paper as it was himself, he was therefore responsible for losses that grocutt incurred due to failure to produce the insurance document.
- depositum - naked bailment
	- a delivers goods to b for b to look after, without charge
^1777153154276




## Chapman v. Great Western Rly (1880) 5 QBD 278 #card
- travelling salesman sent goods by rail to a station to be collected by him
- wasnt there to pick them up, stored near the station instead.
- subsequent fire destroying the goods
- firstly locatio operis faciendi - paid for the goods to be sent
- secondly locatio custodiae - goods bailed to another who is paid to look after them
- duty owed under locatio custodiae isnt as high as locatio operis faciendi
	- no requirment to demonstrate negligence in locatio operis faciendi
- locatio operis faciendi
- locatio custodiae
^1777153154277

## ‘The Pioneer Container’ [1994] 2 AC 324 #card
- ship ran into another ship in high fog 
- goods transported lost. 
- clause in original contract of carriage afforded protection to bailees and to sub bailee on any terms.
- sub bailed on favourable terms to sub-bailee in terms of exclusion of liability and so nobody was liable
- had the clause not been in the original contract, then there wad no protection afforded
- exclusion clauses apply
