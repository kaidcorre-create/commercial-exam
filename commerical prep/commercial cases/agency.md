## Reg 2 Commercial Agents Regulations 1993 #card
- commercial agents are self employed intermediaries
- they must have continuing authority to negotiate the sale/purchase of goods on behalf of the named principal
- they cannot be internal parties
- it cannot be on an exchange/market
- it must be for renumeration
- agency must be their primary function
- they are not distributors
^1776206378982

## Regs 3&4 Commercial Agents Regulations 1993 #card
- agents must
	- look after the principal's interests
	- act in good faith
	- make proper efforts to negotiate
	- keep the principal informed
- principal must
	- act in good faith
	- provide necessary docs/info
	- notify the agent if business volume will drop
- most of these duties cannot be excluded (reg 5)
^1776206378991

##  Regs 20 Commercial Agents Regulations 1993 #card

## Keighley, Maxsted & Co v. Durant [1901] AC 240 #card
- unauthorised contract with a corn merchant to buy wheat at a higher price made in the name of the agent
- appellants failed to take delivery of the wheat
- merchant was forced to sell at a later date, at a loss and sued for the difference
- a contract by an agent made in his own name cannot be ratified
- agency through ratification must have named or identifiable principals
	- a contract incomplete without ratification is a contract which was made with the intention of it needing ratification. that is, made by an agent in the name of a third party
	- a contract which has been made in the name of an agent, is of itself complete and therefore cannot be ratified
^1776206379045
