## Fiona Trust v Privalov [2007] UKHL 40 #card
- Rational businessmen intend ALL disputes arising from their relationship to go to the SAME tribunal 
- arbitration clauses construed broadly
- Arbitration clause is SEPARATE and INDEPENDENT from the main contract
- if the main contract is alleged to have been induced by bribery, the arbitration clause still stands and the arbitral tribunal has jurisdiction to determine that question
^1777319870503


## Enka v Chubb [2020] UKSC 38 #card
- power plant in Russia
- fire in 2016 caused $400 million damage 
- Chubb paid out under insurance policy and by subrogation acquired the Russian entity's rights against Enka
- contract had NO governing law clause for the underlying contract AND no governing law clause for the arbitration agreement — but did provide for ICC arbitration in LONDON
- Chubb commenced Russian court proceedings (May 2019). Enka commenced English Commercial Court proceedings (September 2019), seeking an ANTI-SUIT INJUNCTION to restrain Chubb from Russian proceedings and compel London arbitration
^1777319870508

## s.67 Arbitration Act 1996 #card 
- can challenge award in court setting on grounds of jurisdiction
- court can:
- (a)confirm the award,
- (b)vary the award,
- (c)remit the award to the tribunal, in whole or in part, for reconsideration,
- (d)set aside the award, in whole or in part, or
- (e)declare the award to be of no effect, in whole or in part.
^1777319870531

## s.69 Arbitration Act 1996 #card

## Art 5(1) New York Convention 1958 #card
- Grounds for REFUSAL on application of the party against whom enforcement sought (must be PROVEN by that party): 
- (a) party incapacity or agreement invalid;
- (b) party not given proper notice or unable to present case; 
- (c) award beyond scope of submission to arbitration;
- (d) composition of tribunal or procedure not in accordance with agreement or law of seat; 
- (e) award not yet binding, or set aside/suspended by court of seat
^1777319870558


## Art 5(2) New York Convention 1958 #card
- Grounds for refusal the COURT MAY RAISE OF ITS OWN MOTION: 
- (a) subject matter not arbitrable under law of enforcing state;
- (b) recognition/enforcement would be contrary to PUBLIC POLICY of enforcing state
^1777319870562


## Art 6 New York Convention 1958 #card
